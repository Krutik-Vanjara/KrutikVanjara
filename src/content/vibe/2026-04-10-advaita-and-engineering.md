There is something useful in Advaita's non-dual framework for an engineer: the tendency to identify completely with your technical decisions — "my architecture", "my approach" — makes it harder to see clearly when something isn't working.

Shankara's point isn't that the self doesn't exist, but that the boundaries you draw around it are provisional. A useful reminder when debugging someone else's code or reviewing your own after six months.
